About Me

MSc in Econometrics and PhD in Statistics. Full-time researcher mainly interested in statistical methodology, with a particular focus on Bayesian approaches. Obsessed with football as well as statistics.

I am currently a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Biostatistics at the University of California, Los Angeles, where I am working on Bayesian nonparametric methods.

Research Interests

My research focuses on the development of statistical methodologies, with a particular emphasis on Bayesian approaches. I am interested in mixture models, latent processes, and inference for stochastic processes, aiming to provide flexible and robust tools for the analysis of complex data structures. I also work on copula models and applied Bayesian modeling, with applications across a variety of disciplines including economics, finance, social sciences, and biostatistics.
