Short Bio

I earned an MSc in Econometrics (2016) from the University of Rome "Tor Vergata" and a PhD in Statistics (2020) from the Sapienza University of Rome. My academic journey has included international experiences as a Visiting PhD Student at the University of Plymouth, UK (2018), and as a Visiting Assistant Professor at the University of California, Los Angeles, USA (2025), where I focused on Bayesian nonparametric methods, particularly product partition models.

My research centers on statistical methodology, with a special interest in Bayesian approaches.

Research Interests

My research is devoted to the development of statistical methodologies, with a particular focus on Bayesian approaches. I am particularly interested in parametric and nonparametric Bayesian methods for complex models, including mixture models — both static and dynamic, finite and infinite — as well as stochastic processes, such as point processes and multi-state models, and high-dimensional copula models. Recently, my work has expanded towards the development of robust tools for the analysis of complex data structures, such as quantile multi-state regression models, and the exploration of shrinkage priors. In addition, I am involved in applied Bayesian modeling, with applications spanning economics, finance, social sciences, and biostatistics.

About Ross

I grew up in Nocera — known as Nuvkrinum Alafaternum in Oscan and Nuceria in Latin — a historic city nestled between Naples, Salerno, and the Amalfi Coast. I owe much to Nocera, especially my enduring passion for Nocerina, the local football team that I proudly support in every division.

Beyond statistics, I am passionate about social and economic sciences, as well as wines, football, hiking, electronic music, and dogs. Dogs, more than a passion, are a true obsession. Among the many beloved dogs, I must mention Trilli, my little angel wagging her tail among the clouds, and Carlotta, who stole my heart in just a few seconds. Moreover, I deeply care about civil and social rights, which, sadly, cannot be taken for granted nowadays.
